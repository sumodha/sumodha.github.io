# Problem
Air Alliance Houston currently operates a community dashboard that reports neighborhood air quality data, but this dashboard is **hard to navigate and very inaccessible**; residents often fail to visit the dashboard online to access their (potentially life-saving) analytics. This leaves many **neighbors at risk to pollutants that may directly impact their health**. Furthermore, residents have no way of automatically staying up to date with current data, and instead have to manually monitor the dashboard.  

# Solution
In replacement of their old platform, our team created dashboard that **aggregates live air quality data from ~40 monitors across Houston neighborhoods in need**. The system allows residents to **subscribe to SMS/MMS alerts** when pollutant levels exceed health thresholds, and integrates data analytics and mapping tools. 

# Key Features
- Dashboard displaying **real-time readings directly from 40 air monitors** and **2 external sensor feeds** (PurpleAir and Sensit) 
- **User subscription system** to register contacts with phone numbver, location fields and alert preferences with automatic region inference from latitude/longitude or address 
- Manage **SMS/MMS alerts via Celery tasks** with rate limiting, cooldown handling, and pause/unpause controls
- Send SMS/MSS alerts via **Twilio**
- **JWT-protected admin login** for text blasts and global and region-scoped alert pause/unpause 
- **Dead-letter logging** and support for inspecting failed Celery tasks  
- **Pollutant details** (name, level, and monitor location) displayed on an **interactive map**
