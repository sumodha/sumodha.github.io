# Introduction 
AERO (10143) is **Harmony School of Innovation's FTC robotics team**.

## WHY ROBOTICS?
During my sophomore year of high school, my friend, Lauren, introduced me to our school's robotics club. At the time, I was focused on pre-med related activities, but I figured it could be worthwhile to try something new. On a whim, I applied for the programming team. 

At our introductory meeting, I met our team lead and the rest of the members and I immediately felt out of place. Everyone seemed to have programming experience, and I had none. That evening, I went home and searched "Java basics for beginners." The first result was a 15-minute video covering Java fundamentals: loops, conditionals, primitives, and objects. By our next meeting, I felt "ready". Our team lead had also put together weekly notes on Java basics, which reinforced what I'd already learned while filling in the gaps. Over the course of that semester, I picked up Java through hands-on coding problems. Looking back, I didn't really understand everything that Java had to offer, but I understood the logic of how things fit together, and that was enough to keep me going.

From that point on, **I absolutely loved programming and robotics**, as a whole. The late nights debugging, the scramble to get everything working before competition, and the feeling of finally watching our robot perform. All of it made the effort worth it. **I continued to be apart of robotics for all of high school.**

Bow Bot is the robot we built during our senior year for the First Tech Challenge (FTC) Centerstage season. This project holds a special place for me, not just because of what we built, but because of **who I built it with.** The people on this team have inspired me so much and have made me into the person I am today. I am so glad I get to call them my best friends!
