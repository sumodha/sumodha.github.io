# Introduction 
Rice AR/VR is **Rice's only Augmented Reality and Virtual Reality Organization**. Our mission is to immerse our members in the dynamic world of extended reality including augmented reality, virtual reality and mixed reality. We are dedicated to **fostering a community of innovators, creators, and explorers** who are passionate about pushing the boundaries of perception and interaction. 

## MOTIVATION
In freshman year of college, I was apart of the NASA SUITS team (see more about my work [here](https://sumodha.github.io/portfolio/nasa-suits-2025)), under the Rice AR/VR organization. At the end of year, after the challenge was over, I knew that I wanted to take a larger role in the club! The current presidents, Ben and Yining, wanted to dedicate more of their time to their startup, Norra, so they were looking for students to run the organization. They reached out to Israel, Cameron and me since we had played a significant role in the NASA SUITS project (doing the development work!). **The three of us knew we wanted to continue the club because of how impactful the NASA SUITS project was!** So, we became co-presidents of the organization! 

# Role & Responsibilities
As president of Rice AR/VR, I, alongside my co-presidents, Cameron and Israel...
- managed a cross-functional team of **25+ developers, ui/ux designers and human factors researchers for the NASA SUITS project** (see more of that [here](https://sumodha.github.io/portfolio/nasa-suits-2026))
- led **end-to-end product lifecyle** for our pressurized rover design solution by maintaining **comprehensive
documentation** including requirements specs, development guide (frontend, backend, github), sprint backlogs and risk mitigation and using **Agile methodologies**
- planned and faciliated **15+ sprints for developers, designers and researchers**, ensuring that they followed our set timeline and continued to update us on their progress and any blockers that they faced
- hosted **7+ workshops and industry events** with **35+ attendees each** to promote AR/VR throughout Rice campus
- attended **weekly meetings with NASA stakeholders** to update them on the progress of our work and clarify design requirements  
- particpating in bi-weekly meetings with **our faculty advisor, Dr. LiKamWa**, to gain advice on leading teams and give updates on our progress 
- cordinated meetings with **our NASA mentor, Alex Kanelakos**, to gain constructive feedback on our designs and learn more about how pressurized rover works at NASA
