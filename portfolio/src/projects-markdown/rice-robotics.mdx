# Introduction
Rice Robotics is Rice's **one and only robotics organization**. The goal of the club is to encourage students to collaborate on projects to develop their skills in engineering for industry development and research, working together to prepare for **showcase and competitions** throughout the year!

## WHY RICE ROBOTICS?
**I love robotics!** I was part of my high school's robotics team (see more about that [here](https://sumodha.github.io/portfolio/bowbot)), and I knew I wanted to continue in college. **When I discovered the Rice Robotics Club at the fall club showcase, I knew I had to be apart of the team!** More specficially, I was really interested in the lunar rover project that they were working on. I attended the first general body meeting, but as I was still adjusting to college life, I struggled to find the time to stay involved.

Later that fall, once I'd settled in, I joined Rice's Machine Learning Club to build my machine learning skills. As it turned out, they were collaborating with the Robotics Club on the very same lunar rover project, so this felt like the perfect way back in. After joining the ML team, I realized I wanted a bigger role on the robotics side and asked to join that team as well. 

During my first year, I worked alongside Israel and our simulation team lead, Cameron, on the **Gazebo-based simulation environment**, while also collaborating with Eric on the **machine learning algorithm** side. During this year, I also **learned ROS2 for the first time**! The following year, I stepped into the role of communications lead, where I **built a low-latency base station streaming platform for four rover cameras**.
