# Introduction
OASUS is **Rice's Office of Academic Support for Undergraduate Students**. They provide many student resources, such as PAL study groups, peer educator development programs, a testing center, individual learning consultations, and drop-in study. **Drop-in study is a peer-led tutoring program for students at Rice** that allows them to study with classmates and trained peer educators, ask questions about homework, review for exams, build on foundational knowledge in the class, or listen to other students talk about course content. 


## WHY MATH 212 PEER EDUCATOR?
To be quite frank, MATH 212 was one of the most challenging math classes that I have taken. **MATH 212 forced me to think about calculus in way that I had never thought before.** In Calculus 1 and 2, I used my intuition to get through problems; even when I did not know the exact process for solving the problem, I could reason through it by coming up with visualizations in my head. However, for Calculus 3, I had a hard time with using that same technique. How do I visualize in 3-D? 

It took many practice problems, determination, and using 3-D Desmos over and over again to use the same technique I had used before for Multi. More than anything, what really helped me was the large amount of Multivariable Calculus videos online. 

During winter break of my second year, Matt Resse, Associate Director of the Office of Academic Support for Undergraduate Students, reached out over email about the opportunity to be peer educator for MATH 212. **I had struggled so much in that class and that made me more even more determined to apply.** I applied, had a short interview about my previous tutoring experience and scenario questions, and then, got the position! I was super thrilled! 


# Roles & Responsibilities
As a **peer educator for MATH 212**, I...
- tutored fellow peers on **key concepts and ideas** in Multivariable Calculus through bi-weekly drop-in sessions
- guided students through homework assignments and problems, using techniques such as 
    - **scaffolding**
    - **adaptive tutoring**
    - **modeling effective learning**
    - **asking guiding questions**
    - ... in order to ensure that students were **actively learning** and not just passively understanding
- attended **training and professional development workshops**, which focused on...
    - building **inclusive learning communities**
    - learning how to learn (**metacognitive awareness**)
    - ethics and professionalism
    - creating **meaningful learning experiences** 
    - working with **groups**
    - providing **inclusive support for the whole student**
    - working with **learning preferences**
- planned and hosted three exam review sessions (two for midterms, one for finals), **attended by 200+ students each**
