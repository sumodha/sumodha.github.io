# Introduction
COMP 140 (Computational Thinking) is an **introductory Computer Science class** taught at Rice University. Although it is an intro CS class, it is also a requirement for a variety of other majors, such as Statistics, Computational Applied Mathematics and Operations Research, Bioengineering, etc. It **focuses on using computation to enhance problem solving abilities** and does not require any background in programming; in other words, the class is NOT about programming, rather it is about getting used to thinking through problems computationally. It is taught in a **flipped classroom style**, where students watch short videos and do some short readings before each class, and then in class each day, students work in groups of two or three students on in-class exercises to help them better learn that material and get experience applying it. 

## WHY COMP 140 TA?
I took COMP 140 fall semester of my freshman year. During class time, my in class TA, Anderson, walked our group through the in-class exercises, helped us understand concepts that we did misunderstood or had a difficult time grasping when we were watching the videos and **pushed us to think about the problems in different ways**. Outside of my class, one of the out of class TA's (also coincidentally my o-week father), Mac, walked me through how to write a proper recipe, answered any questions I had about the homework, and guided me through discussion questions in a way that **forced me to think outside of the box**. 

The TA's made the class what it is. I liked the videos that Professor Scott Rixner made, but the TA's were the ones that really helped me **interalize all of the concepts and ideas that were discussed**. I could not imagine the class without the TA's. 

When Professor Dave Johnson reached out about becoming a TA since I had done well in class, I was so excited! **I wanted to foster the same learning that my TA's had shown me.**

# Roles & Responsibilities 
As a **teaching assistant for COMP 140 (Computation Thinking)**, I...
- supported a **400+ class**
- hosted **weekly office hours** 
- **clarified key concepts and ideas** taught during class and in the daily videos
- guided students through homework assignments, **pushing them to think for themselves**

- graded homework assignments and gave **extensive feedback** on what students misunderstood and how to improve 
- assisted with course activities, such as the **A * treasure hunt** (a treasure hunt throughout the Rice campus in order to teach students how A* works)
- attended **weekly meetings** with Professor Johnson and other TA's to review and refresh on course material
