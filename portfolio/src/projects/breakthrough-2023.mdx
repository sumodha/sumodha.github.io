# Introduction
Breakthrough is the **nation’s largest pre-professional teacher program for underserved communities**. It's not just a summer program; it's a **place of community and support**, surrounded by people who are advocating and pushing for your success. 

## BREAKTHROUGH JOURNEY
My story with Breakthrough starts before the summer of 2017, when one of my family friends, Aruna introduced me to the program. At the time, she was a rising ninth grader, who had experienced two years of the Breakthrough program. She said that I was eligible for the program as I was a rising seventh grader and that it was something that she could see me being apart of. 

The application, itself, was quite short, but it really emphasized that Breakthrough was a six year commitment. Every summer until 11th grade, I would be apart of Breakthrough. And even after, there would be ample support and guidance to ensure that I went to college. 

Now, to be quite frank, the thought of giving up my summer to sharpen my writing skills, read a new book and solve math problems wasn't exactly thrilling. But, for whatever reason, I thought it could be a bit fun. I would get to learn something new and I would be doing it alongside Aruna. 

**Long story short, I got in!** That year I was a part of the Georgia Tech Yellow Jackets team (black and yellow... black and yellow...). The following year I was a UT Longhorn; after that, I was a Rice Owl (subtle forshadowing), and then a Yale Bulldog. 

## WHY TEACHING AT BREAKTHROUGH?
When the opportunity arose the summer before senior year to become a teaching fellow, I was more than estactic! **I LOVED Breakthrough and its mission.** More than that, I got to see first hand, how important teaching experiences are to students (since I got to be the student!). In particular, my Pre-Algebra teacher (Yellow Jackets team), Alex, poured so much joy in his classes, whether it was doing an exam review by playing trashketball or wearing a neon yellow suit on dress up days; he made me want to spread the energy that he gave me. **I knew that I HAD to teach here.** 

# Roles & Responsibilities
As a teaching fellow, I taught **2 pre-algebra classes with 10+ students**. But, that's not all, I also...
- particpated in an **intensive, week long training** to learn how to plan lessons, manage the classroom, build my communication skills and have difficult conversations about disparities in education  
- recieved **feedback and guidance from my curriculum head**, Tasha Williams, to create better, more comprehensive **lesson plans**, as well as, better lead the classroom
- attended **professional development workshops** to improve my teaching skills and set goals throughout the summer to continue growing as a teacher
- planned and led **team bonding exercises** to faciliate team spirit and improve teamwork skills between the students
- **supported students** by making sure that they were keeping track of their homework assignments and pushing them to advocate for themself and their learning
- joined **staff meetings** to stay updated with administrative tasks
